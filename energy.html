MY GREEN LIFE

ENERGY SAVING TIPS

As the global population increases the demand for energy around the world increases. This demand can be offset by implementing renewable energy sources into our lives and ensuring we minimise our own personal energy devices for example by switching off all applicances when they are not in use.

We use energy in almost everything we do, such as cooking, bathing and using the Internet.
One hour’s worth of energy from the sun could power the Earth for a year - we just need to figure out the best way to harness it.
2.3 billion people are joining the planet by 2050 – this will require a 60-70% increase in global food production

Switch it off

Electricity still runs when the switch has been left on, even when the receiving device is not plugged in.

Why does this matter?

  • You are using up energy from the grid that could be used elsewhere
  • Using energy costs money - turn it off to save money!
Additional Tip Turn on the timer for your heater to turn on just as you get home from work/school. This way you won't be wasting energy by leaving it on all day!

Layer up

We know how gruelling the cold winter months may be, but it's definitely worthwile to sometimes ditch the heating and putting on extra layers instead.

Additional Tip Make sure your central heating is on a timer to turn off while you sleep but switch back on as you wake up!

Get smart

There are a variety of things you can install in your home to make it as efficient as possible, saving you hundreds of pounds in the long run.

  • Smart thermostats are also more efficient because they only heat rooms which are being occupied.
  • Double glazed windows minimises heat lost to from the house to the environment.
  • Buy efficient appliances - the most efficient appliances are those with a rating of A+, which uses almost 40% less energy than a B-rated appliance

Additional Tip Installing a smart thermostat allows you to track your energy usage making it easier for you to make adjustments to your energy usage based on what you can already see you're using!
